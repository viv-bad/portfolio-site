About me

I hold a PhD from the Department of Chemistry, University of Cambridge, and two further Master's degrees in Engineering and Physics and Pure Chemistry. Having spent 8 years in a laboratory, specialising in renewable energy technology, I realise the importance (and lack) of good software in the renewable energy landscape. My first exposure to programming was to write scripts to automate mt data processing and visualisation in Python, which was key to my research progression. I have therefore made it a priority of mine to bridge my practical, hands-on expertise in the lab with software development, with the hopes of building highly user-friendly, beautifully designed and data-driven software to help aid the development of products in the renewable energy industry. Since completing my PhD, I have completed various courses and have built projects using various technologies which you can find in my skills section.
